Title: Rendering open street maps

Summary: generating graphics from openstreetmap.com data with MetaPost and ConTeXt.

Full text of article: available to TUG members (join TUG); will be publicly available after the next issue is published.

Author: Hans Hagen

Publication: TUGboat volume 42, number 3 (2021), pages 258-266

DOI (this page): 10.47397/tb/42-3/tb132hagen-openstreetmap
(previous doi - next doi)

Categories: Graphics - ConTeXt

Difficulty: Intermediate Plus

Abstract: At the 2021 ConTeXt meeting I did a presentation about rendering so-called open street maps with MetaPost. I will roughly describe what it takes and show some TeX, MetaPost, Lua, and XML.

These are maps available on the web and in some mobile applications made from contributions by (public) organizations, governments, and volunteers. On the web these maps are rendered efficiently from cached tiles (bitmaps).
