Title: On typesetting an English–Japanese book

Summary: discusses bilingual issues with fonts (fontspec), page layout (titleps), PDF bookmarks, and more.

Full text of article: publicly available now.

Author: Antoine Bossard

Publication: TUGboat volume 42, number 2 (2021), pages 147-151

DOI (this page): 10.47397/tb/42-2/tb131bossard-engjpn
(previous doi - next doi)

Category: Multilingual Document Processing

Difficulty: Intermediate

Abstract: Improving the English skills of non-native undergraduate students has important implications and can often be directly linked to students’ futures, particularly in the IT field. The edition of a bilingual lecture textbook is thus meaningful, notably by considering English as the “major” book language and students’ mother tongue the “minor”, supporting one. Yet, from a TeXnical point of view, this is far from being trivial. In this article, LaTeX methods are given together with guidelines to support the realisation of a bilingual textbook. The especially technically demanding English–Japanese scenario is considered.
