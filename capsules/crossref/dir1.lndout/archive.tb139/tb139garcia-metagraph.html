Title: Nodes and edges with MetaPost: The MetaGraph environment

Summary: general-purpose programming with data sets of nodes and edges.

Full text of article: publicly available now.

Author: Federico García De Castro

Publication: TUGboat volume 45, number 1 (2024), pages 117–124

DOI (this page): 10.47397/tb/45-1/tb139garcia-metagraph
(previous doi - next doi)

Category: Graphics

Difficulty: Advanced

Abstract: The aim of this article is to present MetaGraph, a set of MetaPost macros and utilities developed over the last couple of months as an open-ended environment for drawing graphs (in the sense of “nodes and edges”), and intended to complement external graph analysis engines with the versatility of programmatic formatting.

After a quick glance at MetaGraph’s capabilities through three demo graphs, the sections below offer a general description of the system, highlighting the data vs. procedure approach that makes it different from the plotting routines typically available in those external engines—with a special mention of TikZ—and offering a general ‘feel’ for what this approach entails and permits.
