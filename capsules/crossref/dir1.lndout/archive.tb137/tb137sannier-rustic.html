Title: A Metafont for rustic capitals

Summary: fully parameterized Metafont design using Vergilius Vaticanus as a reference.

Full text of article: available to TUG members (join TUG); will be publicly available after the next issue is published.

Author: Victor Sannier

Publication: TUGboat volume 44, number 2 (2023), pages 209-212

DOI (this page): 10.47397/tb/44-2/tb137sannier-rustic
(previous doi - next doi)

Category: Fonts

Difficulty: Intermediate

Abstract: Littera capitalis rustica (literally country capital letters) are a script of the Latin alphabet, the earliest examples of which date back to the first century CE, notably on election posters in the city of Pompeii. They were gradually regularised in the fourth and fifth centuries and remained in use until the ninth and tenth centuries, but mostly for titles and distinctions [1].
