Title: The LaTeX template generator: How micro-templates reduce template maintenance effort

Summary: separating writing from knowledge of packages, classes, tools.

Full text of article: available to TUG members (join TUG); will be publicly available after the next issue is published.

Author: Oliver Kopp

Publication: TUGboat volume 44, number 2 (2023), pages 203-207

DOI (this page): 10.47397/tb/44-2/tb137kopp-microtemplates
(previous doi - next doi)

Category: LaTeX

Difficulty: Intermediate Plus

Abstract: Scientific findings are published by different publishers. These provide different templates. These differ in the documentation and packages provided. For example, hyperref or microtype are mostly not included or not configured properly. Furthermore, there is a demand for minimal examples in the body of the paper. For instance, how to typeset a listing with line numbers and hyperlink to that line number. These minimal examples should appear in any paper template. If the minimal example is updated, how can various paper templates be updated automatically? The “LaTeX Template Generator” is one answer to this question. It uses “micro-templates” to create full-fledged paper templates containing the same configurations for popular packages. Thus, it reduces the maintenance effort of LaTeX templates.
