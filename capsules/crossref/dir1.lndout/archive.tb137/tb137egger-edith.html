Title: New dimensions: Edith and Tove

Summary: two new metric dimensions and a device with which to determine them.

Full text of article: publicly available now.

Authors: Willi Egger, Hans Hagen, Edith Sundqvist, Mikael P. Sundqvist

Publication: TUGboat volume 44, number 2 (2023), pages 180-185

DOI (this page): 10.47397/tb/44-2/tb137egger-edith
(previous doi - next doi)

Category: ConTeXt

Difficulty: Intermediate

Abstract: We need some female units that correctly stay within the metric domain! In order to convince users to drop the inch the first new unit somewhat relates to it: one Edith (es) is the median of the widths of thumbs of BachoTeX 2023 attendees. One can argue that this is somewhat arbitrary and indeed it is. In order to get a decent value we use a discrete measurement device that groups thumbs into 15, 20, 25 and 30 mm intervals. A 10mm interval is unlikely to get many hits unless the TeX ecosystem suddenly became very easy to use and toddlers get interested in it as a game.

Just like a centimeter has a smaller companion in the millimeter, the Edith has a companion Tove (ts). In terms of points, one Tove is 7.11317pt, while an Edith is 71.13177pt.
