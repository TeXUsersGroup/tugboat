Title: Metafont/MetaPost and a complex Indic script: Malayalam

Summary: implementing an aesthetic Unicode-compliant Malayalam font, with many illustrations.

Full text of article: publicly available now.

Authors: CV Radhakrishnan, KV Rajeesh, KH Hussain

Publication: TUGboat volume 44, number 2 (2023), pages 297-314

DOI (this page): 10.47397/tb/44-2/tb137radhakrishnan-malayalam
(previous doi - next doi)

Category: Multilingual Document Processing

Difficulty: Intermediate Plus

Abstract: Malayalam is an Indic script with numerous shape-shifting characters. We explore a reusable component-based design for Malayalam fonts, and develop them using Metafont/MetaPost [6,1] to assemble the characters. We discuss the paradigm shift from GUI design tools to ‘code-based’ design of shapes and glyphs, even by non-coders, and the advantages and challenges of using Metafont/MetaPost to develop an OpenType font for a complex script. Finally, the progress made by our small team is shared.
