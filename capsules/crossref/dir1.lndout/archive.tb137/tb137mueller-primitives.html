Title: An HTML/CSS schema for TeX primitives— generating high-quality responsive HTML from generic TeX

Summary: discussion of translating TeX primitives in the RusTeX engine.

Full text of article: available to TUG members (join TUG); will be publicly available after the next issue is published.

Author: Dennis Müller

Publication: TUGboat volume 44, number 2 (2023), pages 275-286

DOI (this page): 10.47397/tb/44-2/tb137mueller-primitives
(previous doi - next doi)

Category: Electronic Documents

Difficulty: Advanced

Abstract: I present a schema for translating TeX primitives to HTML/CSS. This translation can serve as a basis for (very) low-level TeX-to-HTMLconverters, and is in fact used by the RusTeX system—a (somewhat experimental) implementation of a TeX engine in Rust, used to convert LaTeX documents to HTML—for that purpose.

Notably, the schema is accurate enough to yield surprisingly decent (and surprisingly often the “exactly right”) results on surprisingly many “high-level” LaTeX macros, which makes it adequate to use in lieu of (and often even instead of) dedicated support for macros and packages.
