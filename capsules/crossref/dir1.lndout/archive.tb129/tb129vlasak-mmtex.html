Title: MMTeX: Creating a minimal and modern TeX distribution for GNU/Linux

Summary: a small TeX distribution with LuaTeX and OpTeX that integrates easily on modern systems.

Full text of article: available to TUG members (join TUG); will be publicly available after the next issue is published.

Author: Michal Vlasák

Publication: TUGboat volume 41, number 3 (2020), pages 343-345

DOI: 10.47397/tb/41-3/tb129vlasak-mmtex
(previous doi - next doi)

Category: Software & Tools

Difficulty: Intermediate Plus

Abstract: MMTeX stands for “minimal modern TeX” and is a simple, small and legacy-free distribution of TeX for GNU/Linux. It has the form of a installable package, offers full functionality of OpTeX and plain LuaTeX formats, allows use of system fonts and resources in external texmf trees.

This article explains my motivation for creating it, describes some aspects of a distribution in general and how they are handled in MMTeX. My goal is to show that things can be done simply, and that TeX can integrate better into a Unix system and not be the odd one out.
