Title: Expanding hyphenation patterns across Slavic languages

Summary: converting public word lists to speech and IPA for cross-language hyphenation.

Full text of article: available to TUG members (join TUG); will be publicly available after the next issue is published.

Author: Ondřej Sojka

Publication: TUGboat volume 45, number 2 (2024), pages 268–270

DOI (this page): 10.47397/tb/45-2/tb140sojka-slavic
(previous doi - next doi)

Category: Multilingual Document Processing

Difficulty: Intermediate Plus

Abstract: So far, TeX hyphenation patterns, even for related languages, have been developed separately for each language, splitting scarce human resources. As languages develop and (especially) English terms creep into formerly monolingual texts, hyphenation patterns, especially for medium- and low-resource languages which often lack quality generated patterns, are due for an update. In this article, we explore the possibilities for transfer learning of hyphenation rules between related Slavic languages.

We present new hyphenation patterns for multiple Slavic languages, developed using transfer learning from various sources.
