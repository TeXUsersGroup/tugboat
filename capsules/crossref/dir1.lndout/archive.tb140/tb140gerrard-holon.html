Title: Holon programming regained

Summary: unearthing the predecessor(s) of Knuth's WEB system of literate programming.

Full text of article: available to TUG members (join TUG); will be publicly available after the next issue is published.

Author: Mitchell Gerrard

Publication: TUGboat volume 45, number 2 (2024), pages 196–200

DOI (this page): 10.47397/tb/45-2/tb140gerrard-holon
(previous doi - next doi)

Category: Literate Programming

Difficulty: Intermediate

Abstract: One of the main inspirations for literate programming was a technical report entitled Holon Programming: A Survey, by Pierre-Arnoul de Marneffe. It was privately circulated among computer scientists in 1973. The document thereafter became a Borgesian mythical book, existing only in citations by Knuth. This article narrates the search-and-rescue mission of this rare book, and highlights a few of its innovations. The full report is available at github.com/holon-scribe/holon-programming.
