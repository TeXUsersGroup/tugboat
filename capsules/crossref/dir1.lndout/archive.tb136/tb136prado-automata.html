Title: An introduction to automata design with TikZ's automata library

Summary: typesetting finite automata (state machines), including loops and conditionals.

Full text of article: available to TUG members (join TUG); will be publicly available after the next issue is published.

Author: Igor Borja

Publication: TUGboat volume 44, number 1 (2023), pages 102-107

DOI (this page): 10.47397/tb/44-1/tb136prado-automata
(previous doi - next doi)

Category: Graphics

Difficulty: Intermediate Plus

Abstract: This article is a quick introduction to TikZ’s automata library, used for the design and typesetting of finite automata in LaTeX. It also explores the use of TeX loops and conditionals to automate the generation of images that follow noticeable patterns. TikZ itself is a package used for generating a variety of figures—from geometry configurations to graphs and automata—allowing for more control over image editing and quality. Although the package is very versatile, its uses for designing automata will be the primary topic of this article.
