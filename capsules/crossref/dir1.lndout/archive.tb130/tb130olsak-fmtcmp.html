Title: Comparison of OpTeX with other formats: LaTeX and ConTeXt

Summary: differing concepts, implementations, usage of three formats.

Full text of article: available to TUG members (join TUG); will be publicly available after the next issue is published.

Author: Petr Olšák

Publication: TUGboat volume 42, number 1 (2021), pages 44-49

DOI: 10.47397/tb/42-1/tb130olsak-fmtcmp
(previous doi - next doi)

Categories: Surveys - Macros

Difficulty: Intermediate Plus

Abstract: OpTeX [1] was introduced in an article [2] in the previous issue of TUGboat. It is a macro package that creates a format for LuaTeX. Its features are comparable with other formats like LaTeX or ConTeXt. One may ask why use a new format, particularly when it requires a different markup syntax? I try to answer this question here. I present a comparison among the LaTeX, ConTeXt, and OpTeX formats, from various points of view.
